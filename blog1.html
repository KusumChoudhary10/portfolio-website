From Homes to Gutters: How Convenience Is Killing India’s Streets

By a girl who just wanted to walk without falling I live in Wagholi, Pune—a truly beautiful place. Behind my society, there’s a hill that glows golden during
sunset. Inside, the buildings surround a four-floor parking tower, and the roads are smooth, wide, and newly
made.
But as beautiful as everything looks on the outside, the problems are hiding in plain sight.
In our parking area, there’s a constant drainage issue. Not because the system is bad—but because
people throw wrappers, plastic, and all kinds of waste straight into the drains. Every time it rains, the water
backs up. And every time, someone says, “Arre, drain clog ho gaya,” as if it’s a surprise.
It’s not. It’s the result of everyday shortcuts and laziness.
